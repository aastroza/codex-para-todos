# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Codex para todos serves Spanish-speaking learners, professionals, educators, students, and curious non-programmers who want to understand what they can do with Codex in practical work. They arrive through a community, a workshop, a shared link, or a newsletter, often with interest but without confidence that agentic coding tools are meant for them.

## Product Purpose

The site introduces the community, publishes learning notes, shares workshop material, and collects newsletter subscribers. Success means a visitor quickly understands that Codex can be learned without being a programmer, trusts the tone, joins the community, reads a post, or subscribes for future workshops and resources.

## Brand Personality

Clear, close, practical. The brand should feel fresh and modern, with the calm confidence of OpenAI Codex materials, but warmer and more community-oriented. It should invite people in without lowering the bar.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI SaaS landing pages, academic stiffness, startup hype, heavy sales copy, and decorative "AI magic" tropes. Avoid layouts that feel like a template more than a real learning community. The visual reference is the OpenAI Codex developer experience: restrained, crisp, structured, technical without being cold.

## Design Principles

1. Start with the invitation: make the community and learning promise obvious in the first screen.
2. Show the artifact: slides, posts, photos, and event links should feel like real proof that the community is active.
3. Keep the path simple: joining, reading, and subscribing should always be one obvious action away.
4. Be technical without gatekeeping: borrow the clarity of developer documentation, but keep the voice human and accessible.
5. Polish through restraint: use strong type, generous spacing, clean rhythm, and a few memorable details instead of decorative clutter.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus, responsive mobile layouts, readable Spanish text, and reduced-motion alternatives for motion. The site should work for visitors who are new to Codex, scanning quickly, or reading from a phone after an event.
